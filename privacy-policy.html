Privacy Policy

Ostinote — Spaced Repetition Flashcards
Effective Date: March 27, 2026

Ostinote ("we," "our," or "the app") is a spaced repetition flashcard application built by an independent developer. Your privacy matters to us. This policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and the choices you have.

1. Information We Collect

Account information. If you choose to sign in with Google or Apple, we receive your name and email address from the authentication provider. This information is used solely to identify your account.

User-created content. When you are signed in, your flashcard decks, cards, and study progress are stored in Google Cloud Firestore so you can access them across devices.

Local-only mode. If you use the app without signing in, all of your data stays entirely on your device in the browser's local storage. Nothing is transmitted to our servers.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for a single purpose: to provide and maintain the app's core functionality. Specifically:

  • Authenticating your account
  • Syncing your flashcard data across your devices
  • Enabling spaced repetition scheduling based on your study progress

We do not use your data for advertising, analytics, profiling, or any purpose beyond delivering the app experience.

3. Third-Party Services

Firebase Authentication. We use Google Firebase Authentication to handle sign-in with Google and Apple. Firebase processes your authentication credentials in accordance with Google's Privacy Policy.

Google Cloud Firestore. Your flashcard data is stored on Google Cloud Firestore infrastructure when you are signed in. Data is transmitted and stored using Google's security standards.

AI Answer Grading. The app offers optional AI-powered answer grading through third-party AI providers (OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google). To use this feature, you provide your own API key. Your API key is stored locally on your device only and is never sent to Ostinote's servers. When you use AI grading, your flashcard content is sent directly from your device to the AI provider you have configured, subject to that provider's own privacy policy.

4. Data Storage and Security

Cloud data is stored on Google Cloud infrastructure, which employs encryption in transit and at rest. We rely on Firebase and Google Cloud's security practices to protect your information.

Local data (including API keys and any data stored when not signed in) remains on your device and is managed through your browser's local storage.

No security measure is perfect. While we take reasonable steps to protect your data, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

5. Your Rights

You have full control over your data:

  • Access and export. You can export all of your flashcard data as a JSON file directly from the app at any time.
  • Deletion. You can delete your account and all associated cloud data from the Settings page within the app. Local data can be cleared through your browser settings.
  • Portability. The JSON export feature allows you to take your data with you if you stop using the app.

6. Cookies and Tracking

Ostinote does not use cookies for advertising, analytics, or tracking. The only cookies or similar technologies present are those required by Firebase Authentication to maintain your sign-in session.

We do not use any analytics services, advertising networks, or tracking pixels.

7. Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal information with third parties. Google (Firebase/Cloud Firestore) acts as our infrastructure provider and processes data on our behalf in accordance with their terms of service.

8. Children's Privacy

Ostinote is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will take steps to delete that information.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. If we make material changes, we will notify you through the app or by updating the effective date at the top of this page. Your continued use of the app after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
